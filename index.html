Most Pet Products Are Sold Without A Safety Review

It's reasonable to assume the products we buy for our pets go through some saftey review before hitting the shelves.

In reality, many everyday pet products are sold without ingredient limits, material testing, or pre-market review. Unless a product makes a medical claim or causes obvious harm later, it often isn't reviewed at all.

That's why things like lead in ceramic bowls, BPA in plastics, and flame retardants in pet beds still show up on store shelves.

The Pet Parent Blueprint exists to help you understand what to watch for, so you can make informed decisions insted of relying on labels or guesswork.
