Nobody's checking pet products before they're sold

Children's toys are tested for lead and hormone-disrupting chemicals before they can be sold. Pet toys aren't.

Ceramic bowls for humans have lead limits. Pet bowls don't.

Human mattresses have flame retardant restrictions. Pet beds don't.

Research has found BPA and phthalates leaching from dog toys. Flame retardants in dogs' blood. Lead warnings on ceramic glazes with no testing requirement for pet bowls.

Most products are probably fine. But the only way to know which ones aren't is to understand what to look for yourself.
